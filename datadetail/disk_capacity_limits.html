Historical Disk Capacity Limits

This table documents the various capacity limitations that have affected disk storage throughout computing history, from the early DOS 528 MB limit to the modern 137 GB ATA specification barrier.

(C/H/S)		 Exact Capacity
(Bytes)		 Description & Technical Details
528 MB DOS 528 MB Limit 1024/16/63 528,482,304 The infamous 504 MiB limit. When the same C/H/S values are used for both BIOS Int 13 calls and IDE disk I/O, both limitations combine. This was a major problem requiring software (Disk Managers), firmware (translating BIOSes), and hardware (LBA) solutions. Formula: 1024 × 16 × 63 × 512
2.1 GB CMOS RAM Limit 4095/16/63 2,113,413,120 Some older BIOSes only allocate 12 bits for the cylinder count field in CMOS RAM, limiting cylinders to 4095 maximum. Formula: 4095 × 16 × 63 × 512
3.2 GB Phoenix BIOS Bug N/A ~3,277,000,000 A specific bug in Phoenix 4.03 and 4.04 BIOS firmware caused system lockup in CMOS setup for drives over 3277 MB capacity. This was a firmware-specific issue rather than a fundamental limitation.
4.2 GB Simple ECHS Limit 1024/128/63 4,227,858,432 Extended CHS (ECHS) or 'Large disk support' doubles heads and halves cylinders until cylinders ≤ 1024. Limited because DOS/Windows 95 cannot handle ≥256 heads. With 16 reported heads: 8192→4096→2048→1024 cylinders, 128 heads max. Formula: 1024 × 128 × 63 × 512
7.9 GB Revised ECHS Limit 1024/240/63 7,927,234,560 Revised ECHS first adjusts heads to 15, then applies doubling algorithm to reach 240 heads maximum (15→30→60→120→240), avoiding the 256-head limitation. Formula: 1024 × 240 × 63 × 512
8.4 GB BIOS Int 13 Limit 1024/256/63 8,455,716,864 Original BIOS Int 13 specification: Maximum 1024 cylinders (0-1023), 256 heads (0-255), 63 sectors/track (1-63). This is the fundamental BIOS limitation that affects DOS and older operating systems. Formula: 1024 × 256 × 63 × 512
8.4 GB Assisted LBA Limit 1024/255/63 8,422,686,720 Assisted LBA uses optimal translation with 255 heads and 63 sectors/track, avoiding 256-head geometries. Uses first suitable value from sequence (16, 32, 64, 128, 255) for heads, then calculates cylinders. Slightly less than theoretical 8.4 GB. Formula: 1024 × 255 × 63 × 512
33.8 GB BIOS Cylinder Overflow 16383/16/63 ~33,800,000,000 Large disks report 16383/16/63 geometry. BIOSes computing actual cylinders by dividing total capacity by (16×63) get >65535 cylinders for disks >33.8 GB, causing BIOS crashes. Solution: BIOS upgrade, disk jumpers, or OS-level handling. Many operating systems also had problems with these large disks.
137 GB ATA Specification Limit 65536/16/255 136,902,082,560 Original ATA specification for IDE disks: Maximum 65536 cylinders (0-65535), 16 heads (0-15), 255 sectors/track (1-255). Uses only 28 bits for sector addressing (2^28 = 268,435,456 sectors). ATA-6 introduced 48-bit LBA to overcome this. First affected: Maxtor 160 GB disks (Fall 2001). Formula: 65536 × 16 × 255 × 512 = 267,386,880 sectors
Important Notes:
  • Geometry Evolution: Hard drives over 8.4 GB report geometry as 16383/16/63, making traditional geometry calculations obsolete.
  • LBA Addressing: Logical Block Addressing (LBA) replaced cylinder/head/sector addressing to overcome these limitations.
  • Modern Systems: Current systems use 48-bit LBA (ATA-6 and later) supporting drives up to 144 petabytes.
  • Operating System Limits: Various operating systems and file systems have their own additional capacity limitations beyond hardware constraints.
  • Sector Size: All calculations assume 512-byte sectors, which was standard for traditional hard drives.
